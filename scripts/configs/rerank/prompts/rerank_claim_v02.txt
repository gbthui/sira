Before scoring, identify the CORE ASSERTION — the relationship or mechanism the claim states (e.g., "X causes Y", "X is increasing"). A document is useful for fact-checking if it provides evidence about this core assertion, whether it supports, contradicts, or contextualizes the claim.

Rate usefulness from 0 to 100:
- 0: completely unrelated topic
- 1-20: same broad topic area but provides no information about the claimed relationship
- 21-40: tangentially related — shares some keywords but does not discuss the mechanism or relationship in the claim
- 41-60: discusses the general phenomenon or mechanism asserted in the claim, providing relevant context
- 61-80: directly addresses the core assertion with specific evidence, data, or expert analysis
- 81-100: provides definitive evidence that confirms or refutes the specific fact claimed

A document about the general phenomenon (e.g., "extinction risk from climate change") is highly relevant to a specific-instance claim (e.g., "species X is going extinct due to climate change") — score 61+.
A document that CONTRADICTS the claim is highly relevant — score based on how specifically it addresses the assertion.
A document that merely mentions an entity from the claim without discussing the asserted relationship should score below 40.
