Given a claim and a document, rate how useful the document is for fact-checking the claim.

Claim: {query}

Document: {document}

Before scoring, follow these steps:
1. List every entity mentioned in the claim (people, places, things, works, organizations).
2. For EACH entity: is this document the reference article for that entity? If yes, score at least 41.
3. Does the document provide evidence that SUPPORTS or CONTRADICTS any part of the claim? A document that contradicts the claim is EQUALLY useful — both are evidence for fact-checking.
4. For "is X a Y?" claims (e.g., "X is a machine", "X is a musician"): if the document defines or describes category Y, it is useful even if it does not mention X specifically. Score at least 41.

Rate usefulness from 0 to 100:
- 0: completely unrelated — shares no entities, concepts, or categories with the claim
- 1-20: peripherally related but provides no evidence for any part of the claim
- 21-40: shares keywords but does not help verify any sub-fact
- 41-60: the reference article for a claim entity, OR a category/class article relevant to the claim, OR provides evidence for one sub-fact
- 61-80: directly addresses the main assertion with supporting or contradicting evidence
- 81-100: provides definitive evidence that confirms or refutes the specific fact claimed

IMPORTANT: A score of 0 means the document has NOTHING to do with the claim. If the document mentions ANY entity from the claim, the score must be above 0. The Grateful Dead article is ALWAYS relevant to a claim about the Grateful Dead — score at least 41.

IMPORTANT: When the claim is FALSE or contains negation (e.g., "X did not have a soundtrack", "X has zero awards"), documents providing evidence that the claim is wrong are HIGHLY useful. The Grease film article mentioning its soundtrack directly refutes "Grease did not have a soundtrack" and scores 81+.

Output only: {{"score": <integer>}}
